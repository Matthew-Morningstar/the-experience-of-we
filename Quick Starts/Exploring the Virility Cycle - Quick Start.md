# Exploring the Virility Cycle: *A Quick Start*

---

The male body was treated as a flatline: constant, unchanging, always ready. But it isn't. Testosterone pulses daily — highest in the morning, declining through the afternoon, replenishing during sleep. It shifts seasonally. And it follows a long arc across the lifetime that changes the body at every decade.

These rhythms shape energy, mood, patience, desire, and capacity — and neither he nor the people who love him usually have any idea this is happening. When energy drops or desire fades, there's no framework. Only confusion, shame, and silence.

Understanding the rhythm changes everything. The shifts stop being mysterious and start being navigable. "He's flat tonight" becomes "testosterone is lower by evening — this is his body, not his feelings about me."

---

## One practice to try

### Name the current state

For one week, build a simple habit: he names his energy level as it is, a few times a day. Not as explanation for anything. Just as honest body reporting.

- **In the morning,** notice: "I'm sharp right now. This is a good window." The morning testosterone peak often brings clarity, physical readiness, and assertive energy.

- **In the afternoon,** notice: "I'm running lower. Patience is thinner." The afternoon decline is real — not a character flaw, but the body's rhythm.

- **In the evening,** notice: "I'm settled. Less drive, more available for connection." Or: "I'm hitting a wall." The evening trough is the body's lowest hormonal point of the day.

- **Share what you notice.** Brief, honest, without drama. "I'm at about 60% right now." "This is a low-energy window." The companion receives it as data, not as complaint.

After a week of this, both people begin to see a pattern. The body isn't inconsistent — it's rhythmic. And once you see the rhythm, you can work with it instead of against it.
