# Exploring the Female Body: *A Quick Start*

---

There is a body in your relationship that has been simultaneously overexposed and underknown. Evaluated relentlessly from the outside. Rarely explored with reverence from the inside. A body shaped by estrogen and oxytocin, designed for the most metabolically demanding work in the mammalian world, carrying a nervous system tuned for relational attunement — and most of the time, neither person in the relationship actually understands what it's doing or what it needs.

She may know her body through its problems. He may know it through its surfaces. But the body itself — its architecture, its intelligence, what it senses about the relational field before the mind catches up — often remains a mystery to both people.

What changes when both people start paying attention is that the body gets to exhale. The body that has been so thoroughly watched finally gets to be *met*.

---

## One practice to try

### Ask a body question

Instead of "How are you feeling?" (which often produces "I'm fine"), try asking a body question. Once today, or once this week, ask:

- **"What's happening in your body right now?"** Not as a medical question. As genuine curiosity about her lived, physical experience in this moment.

- **Listen to the answer.** She might say something about tension, or energy, or a sensation she doesn't have words for. She might be surprised — no one may have ever asked her this.

- **Receive without fixing.** Don't suggest solutions. Don't compare it to what you've heard is normal. Just receive: "Thank you for telling me. I didn't know that was happening."

- **Follow up.** "Where in your body do you feel that?" "Has it been like that all day?" "What does your body need right now?" Simple follow-up questions that show you're actually tracking her experience.

One question. That's all it takes to begin shifting from a relationship where the female body is seen to one where it is *known*.
